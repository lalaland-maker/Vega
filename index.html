Global Education and Digital Learning Challenges

Literacy rates

Key Insights:

Countries in North America, Europe, and parts of Asia show high youth literacy rates, with Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia lagging behind. These disparities highlight socio-economic differences.

Education investments are crucial in improving these outcomes, especially for countries with limited resources and infrastructure, which leads to a struggle in reducing literacy gaps.

Learning poverty with internet access

High Learning Poverty, Low Internet Access:

Countries in Africa have some of the highest learning poverty rates, combined with low internet access. Limited digital resources in these countries make it harder for students to access online education, especially in rural areas. This creates a significant education gap, with many children lacking the skills to succeed.

Internet Access and Wealth:

Wealthier nations like Singapore and Qatar stand out with very low learning poverty rates. These countries have almost complete internet access and high GDP per capita. In contrast, African nations with lower GNI, like Niger and Chad, exhibit both high poverty and low access to digital resources.

Regional Comparisons:

In Europe, many countries benefit from high internet access, which helps reduce learning poverty across the region. Countries like North America also show similar patterns, with low learning poverty due to extensive digital infrastructure and government support for education.
